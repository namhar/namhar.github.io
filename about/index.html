About

I’m Farrukh Rahman, a Senior Applied Scientist at Microsoft and Head Instructional Associate for CS 7643 (Deep Learning) at Georgia Tech. I have over a decade of experience building and deploying machine learning systems across gaming, healthcare, and robotics.

At Microsoft, I work on imitation learning and computer vision for games, developing agents that act from pixel-level observations and deploying models to production-scale environments. Previously, I worked on large-scale anomaly detection at Aetna/CVS Health and on surgical robotics and electromechanical systems at Medtronic.

At Georgia Tech, I lead a team of 20+ teaching assistants for a course serving 900+ students per term and develop curriculum spanning matrix calculus, vision transformers, diffusion models, and generative modeling.

Research

My research centers on building data-efficient embodied AI systems that operate reliably under real-world constraints. I am interested in methods that reduce supervision, improve robustness, and translate effectively from research to deployment.

Data-Efficient Learning — Imitation learning and offline RL methods for improving sample efficiency and stability in high-dimensional environments, including behavior cloning, dataset aggregation (DAgger), inverse/forward dynamics modeling, weak supervision, and self-supervised pretraining.

Embodied Perception & Decision-Making — Learning to act from raw sensory input in dynamic environments by integrating computer vision, temporal modeling, and goal-conditioned policies.

Production-Scale Learning Systems — Bridging research and deployment through scalable training pipelines, principled evaluation, and real-time inference optimization, including hardware-aware tuning and model compression (e.g., pruning and quantization).
